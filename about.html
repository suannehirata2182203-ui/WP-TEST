About Me

The story behind the stadium seats

Home / About

Hey, I'm Marcus Reynolds

I'm 38 years old, I live in Denver, and I've spent the last twelve years of my life in stadiums, arenas, and ballparks across America. What started as a hobby became an obsession, and eventually, it became my career.

I'm not a sports journalist. I'm not a former athlete. I'm just a guy who realized that the experience of attending live sports is something special, something worth understanding deeply and sharing with others.

My Journey

2012

The First Game

Attended my first NFL game: Broncos vs. Steelers at Mile High. Showed up late, sat in the nosebleeds, and had no idea what I was missing by not tailgating. But something clicked that day. The energy of 75,000 people unified behind a common goal was intoxicating.

2015

The Decision

After three years of working as an accountant during the week and traveling to games on weekends, I realized I was only truly happy when I was on the road. I quit my job at a mid-size accounting firm in Denver. My parents thought I'd lost my mind. Maybe I had.

2016

The Conversion

Big Dave at Arrowhead Stadium changed my understanding of tailgating. A stranger handed me brisket that had been smoking since 4 AM, and I understood that the game is only part of the experience. I spent that entire season learning tailgate culture across the NFL.

2017

The First Road Trip

Bought a beat-up Subaru and drove 6,200 miles in three weeks, hitting twelve MLB stadiums. Slept in the car more than I should have, ate stadium hot dogs for breakfast, and cried at Wrigley Field watching the Cubs' first home opener as World Champions.

2019

Going Professional

Started offering guided sports trips to international visitors. What began as helping a few friends from overseas turned into a legitimate business. Turns out, people from other countries are fascinated by American sports culture and willing to pay for authentic experiences.

2020

The Pause

The pandemic shut everything down. No games, no travel, no purpose. I spent eight months at home, writing detailed guides to every stadium I'd visited. Those notes became the foundation for this website.

2021-Present

Back on the Road

When stadiums reopened, I hit the ground running. Completed my NBA arena tour, expanded my MLB collection to all 30 parks, and started this website to share everything I've learned. Now I help dozens of people each year plan their perfect sports trip.

What I Actually Do

These days, my work breaks down into a few areas:

  • Guided sports trips: I organize multi-day experiences for international visitors who want to understand American sports culture beyond just watching a game. We tailgate, we explore cities, we eat at local spots, we learn the traditions.
  • Consulting: Some people just want advice on planning their own trip. I offer one-on-one calls where I help map out itineraries, suggest ticket strategies, and share insider tips.
  • This website: Everything I know, available for free. I believe the more people who experience American sports culture authentically, the better.

A Few Personal Notes

I'm a Broncos fan first and forever. I became a fan as a kid in the Elway era, suffered through the Jake Plummer years, celebrated the Peyton Manning redemption arc, and I'm cautiously optimistic about the current rebuild.

In baseball, I root for the Rockies, though I'll be honest: I've developed soft spots for the Cubs, the Cardinals, and the Mariners based purely on stadium experiences.

I'm not married, I don't have kids, and my apartment is full of stadium memorabilia that would probably concern a therapist. My dog, a German Shepherd named Blitz, comes with me on road trips when the weather allows.

I graduated from the University of Colorado with an accounting degree that I used for exactly four years before realizing spreadsheets weren't my calling. Sometimes the scenic route is the right one.
